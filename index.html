IEEE Published

Conference Paper · Computer Vision & AI

Smart LIP-Reading Using CNN With Time-distributed Layers

From Silent Video to Kannada Text

Visual speech recognition (VSR), commonly known as lip reading, presents a vital alternative for communication in scenarios where audio is compromised or for individuals with hearing impairments. This paper details the design, implementation, and evaluation of an end-to-end system that leverages deep learning to interpret spoken English words and phrases from silent video recordings of a speaker's lip movements. The core of the proposed system is a deep Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture, encapsulated within a TimeDistributed layer to effectively process video frames as a sequence and learn spatiotemporal features. Trained and evaluated on the MIRACL-VC1 dataset, the model achieves high classification accuracy.
